Feelings and imagination are closely connected, which might seem surprising. When we care about one thing, our feelings can easily move to something related to it. But it’s hard for those feelings to switch to things that aren’t connected at all.

If a writer puts characters and events that don’t belong together into a story, they lose the chance to make readers feel strong emotions. This is the only way to truly grab people’s attention and make the story exciting.

Explaining this idea fully would be too complicated and long for now. For now, it’s enough to know that all ideas connect through three main links: things that look alike, things that happen near each other, and things that cause one another.
