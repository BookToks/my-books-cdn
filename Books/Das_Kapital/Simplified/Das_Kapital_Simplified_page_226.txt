These speculators are so careful about the workers' effort that they feel like they should be paid for it. The employer will always try hard to save time and labor. 

The capitalists want the workers they hire to be as productive as possible. They focus almost completely on improving that productivity.

Capitalist production really starts when each business uses a large number of workers at the same time. This way, work happens on a bigger scale and produces a lot more products.
