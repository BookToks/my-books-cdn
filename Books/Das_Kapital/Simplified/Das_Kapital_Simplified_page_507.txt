The serf was freed in that country before he had any legal right to the land. As soon as he was freed, he became a free worker who found his old master waiting for him in the towns. These towns were mostly inherited from Roman times.

Around the end of the 15th century, when Northern Italy lost its top place in trade because of changes in the world market, things started to go the other way. Many town workers were forced to move back to the countryside. This caused a big boost in small-scale farming, especially gardening, like never before.
