On the other hand, Don Quixote already faced the consequences of wrongly thinking that being a knight-errant could work with all kinds of economies.

The writer of "Observations" and S. Bailey say that Ricardo changed the idea of exchange value from something relative to something absolute. But that’s not true. Ricardo showed how things like diamonds and pearls seem to have value when you trade them, and he explained that their real value comes from the human work behind them.

Ricardo’s followers sometimes answer Bailey in a rude and not very good way because they can’t find in Ricardo’s own writing a clear explanation of how value and exchange value are connected.
