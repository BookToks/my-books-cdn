I think there are very few places in the world where this could have happened.

The main speech was given by Hon. Waddy Thompson, who was in charge of education for the county. Around the corner-stone stood the teachers, students, their parents and friends, county leaders—who were white—and all the important white men nearby, along with many black men and women who, just a few years before, had been considered property by those same white people. People from both races were eager to put something special under the corner-stone.

Before the building was finished, we went through some really hard times. Many times, we felt like our hearts were breaking because bills had to be paid, but we didn’t have the money. Maybe no one who hasn’t spent many months worrying and staying up at night trying to build a school without knowing where the money would come from can truly understand how tough it was for us.
