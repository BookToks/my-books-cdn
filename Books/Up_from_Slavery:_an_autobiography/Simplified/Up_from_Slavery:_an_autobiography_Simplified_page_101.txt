Many of them had parents who were old and needed their help. Some were men who had wives they had to support in some way.

The big idea everyone had was to get ready to help the people at home. No one was thinking about themselves. The teachers and officers were really special people. They worked for the students all the time, no matter the season. They seemed happy only when they were helping the students.

When it is written—and I hope it will be—the story about how the Yankee teachers helped educate Black people right after the war will be one of the most exciting parts of this country’s history. Soon, the whole South will understand and appreciate this service in a way it hasn’t yet.
