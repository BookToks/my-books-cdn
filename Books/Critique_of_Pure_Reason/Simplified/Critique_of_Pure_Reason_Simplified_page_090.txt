The last selection is not meant to be a full or careful guide to important and helpful works about how to understand the Critique.

German texts I.

KANT, IMMANUEL. Critik der reinen Vernunft. Riga: Johann Friedrich Hartknoch, 1781. Facsimile edition: London: Routledge/Thoemmes Press, 1994.  
KANT, IMMANUEL. Critik der reinen Vernunft. Second improved edition. Riga: Johann Friedrich Hartknoch, 1787. Facsimile edition: London: Routledge/Thoemmes Press, 1994.

KANT, IMMANUEL. Kritik der reinen Vernunft. Second edition 1787. [Edited by Benno Erdmann.] Kant’s collected writings, published by the Royal Prussian Academy of Sciences, Volume III. Berlin: Georg Reimer, 1911.

KANT, IMMANUEL. Kritik der reinen Vernunft (1st edition). [Edited by Benno Erdmann.] Kant’s collected writings, published by the Royal Prussian Academy of Sciences, Volume IV. Berlin: Georg Reimer, 1911. Pages 1-252.

KANT, IMMANUEL. Kritik der reinen Vernunft. Based on the first and second original editions. Edited by Raymund Schmidt. Third edition, with a bibliography by Heiner Klemme. Hamburg: Felix Meiner Verlag, 1990. (First and second editions, 1926, 1930.)  
KANT, IMMANUEL. Kritik der reinen Vernunft. Edited by Wilhelm Weischedel. Complete works, Volumes III, IV. Frankfurt am Main: Suhrkamp, 1974. (Originally Wiesbaden: Insel Verlag, 1956.)  
KANT, IMMANUEL. Kritik der reinen Vernunft. Edited by Ingeborg Heidemann. Stuttgart: Philipp Reclam Jun., 1966.
