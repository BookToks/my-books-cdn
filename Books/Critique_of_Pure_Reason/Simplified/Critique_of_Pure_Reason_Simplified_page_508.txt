He wrote a special paper about a tricky idea in pure reason. One idea was that the moon spins on its axis because it always shows the same side to the Earth. The other idea was that the moon does not spin on its axis, just because it always shows the same side to the Earth.

Both ideas were right, depending on how you look at the moon's movement.

This is called the Antinomy of Pure Reason. It shows how reason gets interested in these kinds of conflicts.
