He answered: You don’t really give much when you only share your things.

True giving happens when you give a part of yourself.

Because your possessions are just things you hold onto, worried you might need them later.

But what will tomorrow bring to someone who’s too careful, like a dog burying bones in the sand while following travelers to a special place?

And isn’t being afraid of needing something the same as actually needing it?

Isn’t being scared of thirst when your well is full the kind of thirst that can never be satisfied?

Some people give very little of what they have—and they do it just to be noticed, which makes their gifts not truly good.
