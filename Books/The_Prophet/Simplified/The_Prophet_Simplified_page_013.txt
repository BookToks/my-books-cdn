As he walked, he saw men and women far away leaving their fields and vineyards. They were rushing toward the city gates.

He heard them calling his name and shouting from one field to another. They were telling each other that his ship was coming.

He thought to himself: Will the day we say goodbye also be the day we come together?

Will it be true that my night was really my morning?

And what should I give to the person who left his plow unfinished, or to the one who stopped pressing grapes for wine?
