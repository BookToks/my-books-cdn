Some of you say, "Joy is greater than sorrow," and others say, "No, sorrow is greater." But I tell you, they always come together.

They come as a pair, and when one is with you at your table, the other is resting on your bed.

You are like a scale hanging between your sorrow and your joy.

Only when you are empty do you stay balanced and still.

When the treasure keeper lifts you to measure his gold and silver, your joy or sorrow must go up or down.

Then a builder stepped forward and said, "Talk to us about Houses."
