I would tell them to look for it.  

Because they will find happiness, but not just happiness alone; there are seven sisters of happiness, and even the smallest one is more beautiful than happiness itself.  

Have you not heard about the man who was digging in the ground for roots and found a treasure?  

Some of your older people remember happy times with sadness, like mistakes made while drunk.  

But feeling sorry is just confusing the mind, not punishing it.  

They should remember their happy times with thankfulness, like they would remember a summer harvest.  

Still, if feeling sorry makes them feel better, let them feel that way.  

And there are some people who are not young enough to look for happiness, nor old enough to remember it; they avoid all pleasures because they are afraid it will hurt their spirit or offend it.
