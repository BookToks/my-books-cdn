Chapter Summary: The opposite of the 1st Law of Behavior Change is to make it invisible.

Once a habit is formed, it’s hard to forget.

People with strong self-control usually avoid places or situations that tempt them. It’s easier to stay away from temptation than to fight it.

One of the best ways to stop a bad habit is to stay away from what triggers it.

Self-control works only for a short time, not forever.

HOW TO CREATE A GOOD HABIT  
The 1st Law: Make It Obvious  
1.1: Fill out the Habits Scorecard. Write down your habits so you can notice them.  
1.2: Use implementation intentions: say, “I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [PLACE].”  
1.3: Use habit stacking: say, “After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT].”  
1.4: Change your environment. Make good habit cues easy to see.

The 2nd Law: Make It Attractive  
The 3rd Law: Make It Easy  
The 4th Law: Make It Satisfying  

HOW TO BREAK A BAD HABIT  
Opposite of the 1st Law: Make It Invisible  
1.5: Reduce exposure. Get rid of things that remind you of your bad habits.

Opposite of the 2nd Law: Make It Unattractive  
Opposite of the 3rd Law: Make It Difficult  
Opposite of the 4th Law: Make It Unsatisfying
