There were of course the usual namaz2 and the prayers, yet they had none of the solemnity of that dread hour.
 
 But the storm had made me one with the passengers. I had little fear of the storm, for I had had experience of similar ones. I am a good sailor and do not get sea-sick. So I could fearlessly move amongst the passengers, bringing them comfort and good cheer, and conveying to them hourly reports of the captain.
 
 The friendship I thus formed stood me, as we shall see, in very good stead.
 
 The ship cast anchor in the port of Durban on the 18th or 19th of December. The Naderi also reached the same day.
 
 But the real storm was still to come.
