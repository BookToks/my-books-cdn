To her, the holy books were like a locked book she couldn't understand, but the old religion her family followed was enough for her.

The children believed strongly in their father's religion and didn’t take the Swami’s talk seriously. But Kasturbai stopped the conversation right away. 

“Swamiji,” she said, “no matter what you say, I don’t want to get better by drinking beef tea. Please don’t bother me about it anymore. You can talk about it with my husband and children if you want. But I have already made up my mind.”
