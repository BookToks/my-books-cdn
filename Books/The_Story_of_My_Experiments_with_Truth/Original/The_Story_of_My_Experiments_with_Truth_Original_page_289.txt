I had kept my luggage ready. I thanked Superintendent Alexander and started for Pretoria.
 
 I now had a fair idea of the difficulties ahead. On reaching Pretoria I drafted the memorial. In Durban I do not recollect the Indians having been asked to submit in advance the names of their representatives, but here there was the new department and it asked to do so. The Pretoria Indians had already come to know that the officers wanted to exclude me.
 
 But another chapter is necessary for this painful though amusing incident.
