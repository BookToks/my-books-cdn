Of all the paths the Eightfold Path is the best; of all the truths the Four Noble Truths are the best; of all things passionlessness is the best: of men the Seeing One (the Buddha) is the best.
 
 274. This is the only path; there is none other for the purification of insight. Tread this path, and you will bewilder Mara.
 
 275. Walking upon this path you will make an end of suffering. Having discovered how to pull out the thorn of lust, I make known the path.
 
 276. You yourselves must strive; the Buddhas only point the way. Those meditative ones who tread the path are released from the bonds of Mara.
 
 277. “All conditioned things are impermanent” – when one sees this with wisdom, one turns away from suffering. This is the path to purification.
 
 278. “All conditioned things are unsatisfactory” – when one sees this with wisdom, one turns away from suffering. This is the path to purification.
 
 279. “All things are not-self” – when one sees this with wisdom, one turns away from suffering.
 
 This is the path to purification.
 
 280. The idler who does not exert himself when he should, who though young and strong is full of 65 sloth, with a mind full of vain thoughts – such an indolent man does not find the path to wisdom.
