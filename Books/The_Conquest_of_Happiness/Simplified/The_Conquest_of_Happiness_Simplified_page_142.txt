Realizing that you’re not as great as you thought might hurt for a little while, but that pain will end, and you can be happy again.

Our third rule was not to expect too much from other people. In the past, sick women often expected at least one daughter to give up everything to take care of them, even if it meant not getting married.

This is asking too much because it’s unfair—the person helping loses more than the person being helped gains. When you deal with people, especially those close to you, you have to remember they see things from their own point of view, not yours.

No one should have to change their whole life just to help someone else.

Sometimes, people care so much that making big sacrifices feels natural. But if it doesn’t feel natural, they shouldn’t do it, and no one should be blamed for not doing it.
