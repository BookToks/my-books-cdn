He argues, and I agree with him, that Ibsen’s Ghosts is not as good as King Lear. No matter how talented Ibsen was, he could never become Shakespeare. 

Shakespeare’s plays were made from ideas about human dignity, strong emotions, and the vastness of human life. These ideas didn’t exist for Ibsen or the people living at his time. 

God, humans, and nature seemed less important over the years. This wasn’t because modern art wanted to show ordinary, mean people, but because life itself seemed smaller and tougher. This change also helped create realistic art that showed life as it is. 

It’s true that old-style tragedies about kings and their problems don’t fit our time. When we try to tell the same kind of story about an ordinary person, it just doesn’t feel the same.
