They don’t really understand that the people affected by what they do each have the right to their own opinion about the kind of world they want. A person who likes to be in charge is sure that their idea is the right one, and that any different idea is wrong. But just because they believe it doesn’t mean it’s actually true.

Also, sometimes this belief hides the fact that they enjoy causing changes and being the one responsible for them. Besides wanting power, there’s also vanity—wanting to look good—that motivates them a lot. 

A serious person who tries to make laws in Parliament, and I know this from experience, feels shocked when voters think he only wants the fame of having ‘M.P.’ after his name. But after the election, when he thinks about it, he realizes the voters might have been right to be suspicious.
