If his risks help others, we can respect him, but if not, we should only think of him as a little better than a gambler or a drunk.

Real excitement, not the kind that makes people want to escape from life, is a natural part of being human unless bad things have taken it away. 

Young kids are curious about everything they see and hear. The world surprises them all the time, and they love learning—not school stuff, but getting to know the things that catch their attention. 

Animals, even grown-ups, stay curious if they are healthy. A cat in a new room won’t sit down until it has sniffed every corner, hoping to find a mouse. 

A person who hasn’t been deeply hurt will keep their natural interest in the world, and as long as they do, life will be enjoyable unless they are seriously restricted.
