Instead of feeling happy about what he has, he feels bad because others have things he doesn’t. If he can, he tries to take away what others have, thinking it’s just as good as having those things himself. If this feeling gets out of control, it stops people from being excellent or using their special skills in a good way.

Why should a doctor drive a car to see patients when a worker has to walk to their job?

Why should a scientist work in a warm room while others have to deal with bad weather? Why should someone with a rare and important talent be saved from doing their own chores? Envy can’t answer questions like these. Luckily, people also have another feeling called admiration. If we want to make life better, we should try to increase admiration and reduce envy.
