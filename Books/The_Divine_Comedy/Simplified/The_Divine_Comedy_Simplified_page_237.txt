"And the other two my song do the name above!

We kept going further, where the ice was. Another group of people were wrapped in rough clothes, but they weren’t facing down—they were all turned the other way.

They couldn’t even cry, because the tears wouldn’t fall. When sadness hits a wall like that, it turns inside and makes the pain worse. Their tears would gather in clusters, and like a crystal glass, fill up the cup under their eyebrows.

Even though their feelings were frozen and numb, and their faces looked like they didn’t feel anything, I still thought I felt some wind. So I asked, 'My Master, who is making this move?'"
