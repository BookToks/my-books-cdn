When he got there, he turned around in a half-circle to join another fight.  

I, feeling heartbroken, shouted, “Master, tell me who these people are, and if they are all clerks. Some of them have crowns on the left side of their heads.”  

He answered, “All of them were slow to understand things in their first life, so their time here is measured by that weakness.”  

Some say clerks or clergy wear a special haircut called a tonsure as a sign of respect for St. Peter. Others think it marks them as important, like a crown made of hair around their head.  

You can clearly hear their voices bark out when they reach two points in the circle, where their opposite faults lie underneath.
