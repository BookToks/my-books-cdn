Then he went past the end of the little bridge. When he got to the sixth bank, he had to stay strong.

Suddenly, just like dogs jumping on a beggar who stops to ask for money, they came out from under the bridge. They aimed all their hooks at him. But he shouted, “Don’t try to hurt me!

Before your hooks grab me, let one of you come forward so I can hear him. Then we can talk about how you’ll catch me.” They all shouted, “Let Malacoda go first.”

Malacoda stepped forward while the others stayed back. He came up and said, “What do you want?”

“Do you really think, Malacoda, that you can hold me?”
