CHAPTER XI ON INTUITIVE KNOWLEDGE

Many people think that everything we believe should be able to be proven or at least shown to be very likely true. They feel that if we can't give a reason for a belief, then it’s not a good belief.

Most of the time, this idea is right. Almost all the things we believe come from other beliefs that explain why we believe them. Usually, we just forget those reasons or never really think about them.

For example, very few of us stop to ask why we think the food we’re about to eat won’t be poison.

But if someone asked us, we feel like we could find a good reason to explain it, even if we don’t have it ready right away.
