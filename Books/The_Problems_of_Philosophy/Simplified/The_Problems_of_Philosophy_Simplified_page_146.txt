At the end of the last chapter, we learned that things like relationships exist in a way that is different from physical objects, minds, and sensory experiences. In this chapter, we need to understand what kind of existence these things have. We also want to know what objects have this kind of existence. Let's start with that question.

This problem has been around for a long time because Plato, a famous philosopher, first talked about it. Plato’s "theory of ideas" tries to solve this problem, and I think it’s one of the best solutions we have so far. The theory I will explain here is mostly Plato’s, with some small changes that time has shown are needed.
