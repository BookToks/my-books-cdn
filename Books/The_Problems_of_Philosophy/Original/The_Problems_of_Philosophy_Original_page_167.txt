 One way of discovering what a proposition deals with is to ask ourselves what words we must understand — in other words, what objects we must be acquainted with — in order to see what the proposition means. As soon as we see what the proposition means, even if we do not yet know whether it is true or false, it is evident that we must have acquaintance with whatever is really dealt with by the proposition. By applying this test, it appears that many propositions which might seem to be concerned with particulars are really concerned only with universals.
