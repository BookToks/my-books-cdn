We know that the man with the iron mask existed, and many propositions are known about him ; b ut we do not know who he was. We know that the candidate who gets the most votes v/ill be elected, and in this case we are very likely also acquainted (in the only sense in which one can be acquainted with some one else) with the man who is, in fact, the candidate who will get most votes ; b ut we do not know which of the candidates he is, i.e. we do not know any proposition of the form " A is the candidate who will get most votes " where A is one of the candidates by name. We shall say that we have " merely descriptive knowledge " of the so-and-so when, although we know^ that the so-and-so exists, and although we may possibly be acquainted with the object which is, in fact, the so-and-so, yet we do not know any proposition " a is the so-and-so," where a is something with which we are acquainted.
