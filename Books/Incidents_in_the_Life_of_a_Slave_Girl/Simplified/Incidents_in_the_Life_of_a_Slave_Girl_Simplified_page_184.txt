But it was the last place they thought of. Still, there was no place where slavery was around that could hide me better.

Dr. Flint and his family kept trying to get my kids to tell them something they had heard about me. One day, the doctor took them to a store and offered them shiny silver coins and colorful handkerchiefs if they would say where their mother was.

Ellen pulled away from him and didn’t say a word. But Benny spoke up and said, “Dr. Flint, I don’t know where my mom is. I guess she’s in New York. When you go there again, please ask her to come home because I want to see her. But if you put her in jail or say you’ll hurt her, I’ll tell her right away.”
