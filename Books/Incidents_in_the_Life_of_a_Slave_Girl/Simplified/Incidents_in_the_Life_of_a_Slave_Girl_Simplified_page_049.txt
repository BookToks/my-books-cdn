Why should he worry her when she couldn’t help him?

The brave old woman kept working hard, hoping to save some of her other children. After some time, she managed to buy Phillip. She paid eight hundred dollars and came home with the important paper that made him free.

That night, the happy mother and son sat together by the old fireplace. They talked about how proud they were of each other and how they would show everyone that they could take care of themselves, just like they had taken care of others for a long time.

Wella finished by saying, “If someone is willing to be a slave, let them be a slave.”
