James Derham and Lemuel Haynes were important early African Americans. Derham was born into slavery around 1762 but learned medicine from his master and bought his freedom by working as a doctor. Haynes, born in 1753 in Connecticut, was mixed-race and fought in the Revolutionary War. He was the first African American to become a minister in a major Protestant church.

Ira Aldridge grew up in New York City and became a famous actor in London during the 1820s. He also toured all over Europe.

The first National Negro Convention happened in Philadelphia from September 20 to 24, 1831. Delegates came from New York, Maryland, Delaware, Pennsylvania, and Virginia.

Robert Purvis, Sr. and Charles Lenox Remond were important abolitionists. James W. C. Pennington was a former slave who wrote a book called The Fugitive Blacksmith in 1849. Henry Highland Garnet, born a slave in Maryland in 1815, became a well-known speaker and editor who fought against slavery. Alexander Crummell and Frederick Douglass were also key figures in the fight for freedom.

Maria Weston Chapman lived in Boston and worked as an editor for the abolitionist movement. She was friends with William Lloyd Garrison.

James McCune Smith grew up free in New York City and became a well-known lawyer, scholar, and anti-slavery supporter.

John Brown Russwurm, whose name is sometimes misspelled, was a Jamaican editor and abolitionist. He studied in Canada and at Bowdoin College in Maine. Elias Nau was a French minister who taught at Trinity Church in New York City. Anthony Benezet, a French Quaker, started a school for black children in Philadelphia.

Daniel Hale Williams founded Chicago Provident Hospital, the first hospital with a mixed-race staff. He was the first surgeon to successfully operate on a heart. Richard Theodore Greener was the first African American to graduate from Harvard. He worked as a diplomat, teacher, and lawyer.

John Brown Russwurm also went to Bowdoin College, as mentioned earlier.

Mark Hopkins, born in Henderson, New York, was the treasurer of the Central Pacific Railroad Company, which built the first railroad across the United States.
