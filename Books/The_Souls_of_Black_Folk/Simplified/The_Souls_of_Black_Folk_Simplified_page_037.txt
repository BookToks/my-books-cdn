xxxvi Chronology 1961 Publishes Worlds of Color, the third volume of The Black Flame.
 
 Daughter Yolande dies of a heart attack in Baltimore in March.
 
 Invited by Kwame Nkrumah to move to Ghana and revive the Encyclopedia Africana project. Applies for membership in the Communist Party of the United States before leaving for Accra in October.
 
 1962 Receives medical treatment in Accra, Budapest, and London.
 
 Travels to China.
 
 1963 Awarded honorary Doctor of Letters degree by the University of Ghana. Acquires Ghanaian citizenship. Dies in Accra on 27 August, the eve of the civil rights march on Washington, DC.
 
 Buried with a state funeral in Accra.
