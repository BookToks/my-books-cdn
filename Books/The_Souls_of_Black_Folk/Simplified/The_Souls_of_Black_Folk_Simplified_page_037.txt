In 1961, he published Worlds of Color, the third book in The Black Flame series.

His daughter Yolande died of a heart attack in Baltimore in March.

Kwame Nkrumah invited him to move to Ghana to restart the Encyclopedia Africana project. Before leaving for Accra in October, he applied to join the Communist Party of the United States.

In 1962, he got medical treatment in Accra, Budapest, and London.

He also traveled to China that year.

In 1963, the University of Ghana gave him an honorary Doctor of Letters degree. He became a Ghanaian citizen. He died in Accra on August 27, just before the big civil rights march in Washington, DC.

He was buried with a state funeral in Accra.
