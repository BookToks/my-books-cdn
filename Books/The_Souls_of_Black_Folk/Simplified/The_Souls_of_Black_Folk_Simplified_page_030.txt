Here is a list of books and articles about important Black writers and thinkers.

Arnold Rampersad wrote a book called The Art and Imagination of W. E. B. Du Bois in 1976.

Adolph L. Reed, Jr. wrote about Du Bois and American politics in his 1997 book W. E. B. Du Bois and American Political Thought.

Robert B. Stepto studied stories by Black Americans in his book From Behind the Veil, first published in 1979 and updated in 1991.

Eric J. Sundquist wrote an essay called "Swing Low: The Souls of Black Folk" in a book about race and American literature published in 1993.

Alexander G. Weheliye’s 2005 book Phonographies talks about sound and Black culture.

Shamoon Zamir wrote Dark Voices, a book about Du Bois and American ideas from 1888 to 1903, published in 1995.

For more reading, Oxford World’s Classics offers books like Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass, an American Slave by Frederick Douglass, edited by Deborah E. McDowell.

They also have Uncle Tom’s Cabin by Harriet Beecher Stowe, edited by Jean Fagan Yellin.

And Up from Slavery by Booker T. Washington, edited by William L. Andrews.
