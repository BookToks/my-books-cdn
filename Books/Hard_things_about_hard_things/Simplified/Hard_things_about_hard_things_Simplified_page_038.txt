— CHAPTER 3 — THIS TIME WITH FEELING

“I keep moving forward, that’s the only way. You can’t be afraid to fail when you’re trying to be perfect.” —JAY Z, “ON TO THE NEXT ONE”

After I finished selling EDS, I thought the company was doing well, but my shareholders didn’t agree. I had sold all of my customers, all of my income, and the business they understood. Every big shareholder left, and the stock price dropped to 35 cents per share, which was about half of the cash we had in the bank. I realized that no one but me knew how bad things really were, and no one else believed in the future. So, I decided to take the employees away for a bit and try to get them excited about what we could do.

I rented forty rooms at a cheap motel in Santa Cruz and took our last eighty employees there for one night of drinking and one day of explaining the Opsware chance. At the end of the day, I tried to be as honest as I could.

“You’ve heard everything I know and think about our chance ahead. Wall Street doesn’t believe Opsware is a good idea, but I do. I get it if you don’t. Since this is a totally new company and challenge, I’m giving everyone new stock shares today. All I ask is that if you want to quit, do it today. I won’t force you to leave — I’ll help you find a job. But we need to know where we stand. We need to know who’s with us and who we can count on. We can’t afford to slowly lose people. You owe it to your teammates to be honest. Let us know what you decide.” That day, two employees quit. Out of the other seventy-eight, all but two stayed until we sold to Hewlett-Packard five years later.
