ABOUT THE AUTHOR  
BEN HOROWITZ helped start and is a main partner at Andreessen Horowitz, a company in Silicon Valley that gives money to people creating new tech companies. They have invested in popular companies like Airbnb, GitHub, Facebook, Pinterest, and Twitter.

Before this, he started and was the CEO of Opsware, which used to be called Loudcloud. Hewlett-Packard bought it for $1.6 billion in 2007. Horowitz writes about what he has learned from being a computer science student, software engineer, CEO, and investor on his blog, which almost ten million people read. He has also been mentioned in big publications like the Wall Street Journal, the New York Times, the New Yorker, Fortune, the Economist, and Bloomberg Businessweek. Horowitz lives in the San Francisco Bay Area with his wife, Felicia.
