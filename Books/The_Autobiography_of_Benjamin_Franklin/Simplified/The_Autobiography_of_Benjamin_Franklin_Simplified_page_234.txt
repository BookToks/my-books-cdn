Some of the commissioners, after trying out Mesmer’s ideas on themselves, decided it was just a trick meant to fool people who didn’t know any better. Mesmer’s plan to get rich and famous was stopped, and his rude attempt to trick people’s minds failed.

Dr. Franklin finished his important job by helping America become independent. As he got older and sick, he wanted to go back home. He asked Congress to let him return, and in 1785, Mr. Jefferson was chosen to take his place. In September of that year, Dr. Franklin got back to Philadelphia. Soon after, he was picked to be part of the city’s top council and then was elected as its president.

In 1787, when a meeting was called in Philadelphia to make the government stronger by changing the articles of confederation, Dr. Franklin was chosen to represent Pennsylvania as a delegate.
