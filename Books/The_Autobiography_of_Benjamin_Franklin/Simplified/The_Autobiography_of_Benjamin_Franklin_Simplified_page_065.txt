 The governor sent every now and then to invite me to dine with him. I considered this as a very great honor ; and I was the more sensible ofit, as he conversed with me in the most affable, familiar, and friendly manner imaginable.
 
 Towards the end of April, 1724, a small vessel was ready to sail for Boston. I took leave of Keimer, upon the pretext of going to see my parents. The governor gaveme a long letter, in which he said many flattering things ofme to my father ; and stronglyrecommended the project ofmy settling at Philadelphia, as a thing which could not fail to make my fortune.
 
 Going down the bay we struck on a flat, and sprung a leak. The weather was very tempestuous, and we were obliged to pump without intermission ; I took my turn. We arrived, however, safe and sound, at Boston, after about a fortnight's passage.
