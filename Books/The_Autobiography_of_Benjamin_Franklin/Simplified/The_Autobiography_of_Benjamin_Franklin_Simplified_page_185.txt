The design was liked so much by the people of Philadelphia that they quickly raised eight hundred pounds every year for five years to make it happen. At the start of January 1750, three schools were opened: the Latin and Greek schools, the Mathematical school, and the English school.

According to the original plan, a school was also opened to teach sixty boys and thirty girls. This school is now called the Charitable School. Even though the people in charge had money problems, the school has stayed open and full for forty years.

Since each child usually spends three years there, at least twelve hundred kids got most of their education at this school. Without it, many of these children might not have had a chance to learn. Many of the students who went there can now be found...
