In the fall of the same year, Dr. Franklin went to Canada to try to get the people there to join the fight for freedom. But they didn’t want to go against the British government. M. le Roy wrote in a letter attached to Abbé Fauchet's praise of Dr. Franklin that the failure was mostly because of religious fights between the Canadians and their neighbors. Some neighbors had even burned their churches at different times.

When Lord Howe came to America in 1776 with the power to talk with the colonists, he and Dr. Franklin exchanged letters about making peace. Later, Dr. Franklin, John Adams, and Edward Rutledge met with the commissioners to find out what power they had. They could only offer pardons if the colonists gave up. The colonists wouldn’t accept these terms, so the commissioners couldn’t reach their goal.

The big question about independence was just beginning.
