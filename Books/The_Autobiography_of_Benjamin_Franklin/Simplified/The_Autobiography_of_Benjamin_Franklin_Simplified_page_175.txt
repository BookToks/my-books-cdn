People thought it was impossible for an American, seen as less important, to make big discoveries. In 1748, a man named Abbe Nollet mentioned the idea that lightning and electricity might be similar. But he only guessed this and didn’t suggest any way to prove it.

Abbe Nollet even said that Franklin was the first to seriously think about bringing lightning down from the sky using pointed rods in the air. The idea that lightning and electricity are alike is very strong, so it’s not surprising that people noticed it once they started learning about electricity.

Scientists like Dr. Wall and Mr. Gray talked about it when the science was new. But the real credit for making a clear theory about thunderstorms, suggesting experiments to test it, and actually doing those experiments to prove it, goes to Franklin.
