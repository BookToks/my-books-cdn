Before, the only newspaper was the "Boston News Letter." Some of his friends tried to stop him from starting a new one because they thought it wouldn’t work. They believed one newspaper was enough for all of America.

But in 1771, there were actually twenty-five newspapers. He went ahead with his plan, and I helped by delivering the papers to customers after helping to write and print them.

He had many friends who loved writing, and for fun, they wrote short essays for the paper. This made the paper popular and helped it sell more. These friends often came to our house, and I listened to their talks about how people liked their writing.
