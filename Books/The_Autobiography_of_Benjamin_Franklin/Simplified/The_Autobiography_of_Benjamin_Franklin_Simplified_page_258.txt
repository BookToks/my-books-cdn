Here’s a list of books and their authors that you might find interesting.

Some of the titles include "Representative Men" by Ralph Waldo Emerson, "Rubaiyat of Omar Khayyam" translated by Edward Fitzgerald, and "Scarlet Letter" by Nathaniel Hawthorne.

There’s also "Sartor Resartus" by Thomas Carlyle, "Sign of the Four" by A. Conan Doyle, and "Stories from the French" by Guy de Maupassant and others.

Other cool picks are "Strange Case of Henry Toplass" by John W. Postgate, "Study in Scarlet" by A. Conan Doyle, and "Three Men in a Boat" by Jerome K. Jerome.

You can check out classics like "Treasure Island" by Robert Louis Stevenson, "Voyage of the Sunbeam" by Lady Brassey, and "White Company" by A. Conan Doyle.

There are also books like "Wicked Girl" by Mary Cecil Hay, "Wonder Book" by Nathaniel Hawthorne, and "Yellow Aster" by Iota.

This list covers a wide range of stories and styles, so there’s something for everyone to enjoy!
